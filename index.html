Top 5 Movies with the Biggest Mind Twists

Read time: 10 minutes

Oldboy: A man with a 3 year old daughter get kidnapped on her birthday and is help captive for 15 years. Having no idea why and struggling to stay sane, after 15 years he is finally set free. With a suit, a telephone and a drive for revenge, Oh Dae-Su sets out to find the man behind this and to make him pay. This movie is disturbing, shocking and teaches you to mind your own business in life. Plot twist hint: do the math.

"jaw dropped" - Rini, 2024

The Handmaiden: A con man hires a pickpocket to work as a handmaiden to a Japanese heiress in order to seduce her, marry her, and steal her inheritance. The movie is split into 3 parts, each part told by a major character and each part has you wondering what direction the movie is going. In life, the only thing people care about is money, but how can you not when you have none. Plot twist hint: you never know when someone is going to stab you in the back.

Shutter Island: 2 U.S. Marshals are sent to investigate the disappearance of a patient from Ashecliffe Hospital, a mental institution for the criminally insane on Shutter Island. One of the Marshalls is haunted by his wife's death and his experiences in World War II. As the investigation deepens, he realizes he must confront his own dark fears to survive and in doing so he discovers a shocking truth. Plot twist: life is just a game.

The Usual Suspects: A waterfront explosion leaves 27 bodies and $91 million in drug money. The only survivors are Verbal Kint, a crippled con-man, and a Hungarian terrorist who is severely burned. Verbal tells the story of how the events leading up to the explosion began with five criminals being arrested and questioned after a truck hijacking. Plot twist hint: The greatest trick you can ever pull is convincing the world you don't exist.

Atonement: This movie tells the story of a young girl who makes a false accusation that ruins the life of her sister's lover. It explores the consequences of her actions, the impact of war, and the nature of writing. 'How old do you have to be before you know the difference between right and wrong?' Plot twist hint: never lie.
